## Key Insights

### What everyone agrees on

1. **Graph-based, no categories** — universally praised. Every persona said this matches how they actually think.
2. **Excalidraw aesthetic** — the hand-drawn feel resonated across technical and non-technical users. It lowers the perceived barrier to entry.
3. **AI as optional, not required** — standalone-first architecture was a trust signal. Users want control.
4. **Local-first** — privacy and ownership matter across all segments, from students to therapists to VCs.

### Top concerns across personas

1. **Collaboration is the #1 gap** — 14 of 16 personas asked for some form of sharing or real-time editing. This is the biggest revenue opportunity.
2. **Export story is unclear** — users need to get data out (PDF, Markdown, domain formats). Without export, it's a walled garden.
3. **Graph scaling** — multiple personas worried about visual clutter at 100+ nodes. Filtering, zooming, and sub-graph views are essential.
4. **Cross-platform timeline** — Phase 2 (Windows/Linux/Android) feels too vague. Users on mixed ecosystems hesitate to commit.

### Revenue model signals

- **Free tier**: Core app (local graph + manual organization) — this is the hook
- **Pro tier ($8-15/mo)**: Sync + export + voice capture
- **AI tier ($15-30/mo)**: AI relationship suggestions, batch operations, persistent memory
- **Team tier ($15-40/user/mo)**: Real-time collaboration, permissions, shared workspaces
- **Enterprise**: HIPAA compliance, self-hosted backend, audit logs, SSO

### Surprising findings

- The **VC persona** would pay $595-1500/mo — relationship intelligence is extremely valuable in finance
- The **ADHD student** cares least about AI and most about zero-friction capture — the app must accept "garbage input instantly"
- The **therapist** would pay $45-50/mo but only after explicit HIPAA certification — compliance unlocks an entire market segment
- **Templates** were polarizing — some personas (retired hobbyist, teacher) want them; others (engineer, founder) would find them patronizing. Make them optional and community-driven.
